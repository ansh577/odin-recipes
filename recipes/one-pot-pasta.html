One Pot Sausage And Sun Dried Tomato Pasta

Image by timolina on Freepik

Description

Delicious, easy, and perfect for a quick dinner after a busy work night, this one pot pasta just needs one skillet and some basic ingredients. Plus, you get plenty of leftovers for a few more days! This recipe should ideally make 4 servings, but in my experience following the original recipe, it has made 1 serving.

The original recipe is by Beth Moncel on BudgetBytes.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup pasta
  • 2 sausages
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cups good quality chicken broth
  • 1/3 cup sundried tomatoes
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 1/2 cup broccoli florets
  • Pinch of red pepper (optional)
  • 1/4 cup grated parmesan

Steps

  1. Add the olive oil and sausages to the skillet, and cook over medium heat till the sausages brown. Then, slice into medallions. Add the medallions back to the skillet and brown on both sides. Transfer the cooked sausage pieces to a bowl. Drain off till you have 1 Tbsp of fat in the skillet.
  2. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and saute till slightly brown. Add the chicken broth and stir to dissolve the brown bits stuck to the bottom of the skillet.
  3. Add the pasta and sun dried tomatoes to the skillet. You can add a pinch of red pepper at this step if you'd like. Turn the heat up to medium high, stir, then close with a lid.
  4. Once the broth starts to boil, stir, replace the lid, and turn the heat down to low so that the broth simmers. simmer for about 7 minutes, stirring every couple of minutes, or until the pasta is tender and most of the liquid is absorbed. If the pasta is tender before the broth was absorbed, let it simmer for a few more minutes without the lid, till the liquid is thick.
  5. Add the cooked sausage and broccoli back to the pasta, and stir to combine. Transfer to a bowl, sprinkle the Parmesean on top, and serve immediately.
